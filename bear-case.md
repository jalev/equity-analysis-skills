> **Format (follow AGENTS.md rules):** Write narrative paragraphs — not bullet lists. Use markdown tables where numbers are compared across periods or scenarios. Cite as `[FY20XX 10-K, Risk Factors]` — never line numbers. The `→` after a quote must add analytical interpretation, not paraphrase the quote.

Write a rigorous bear case for this company. Assume you are short and need
to convince a skeptical investor committee.

Structure:

1. **Thesis killer** - the single most likely way a long-term holder loses money
2. **Structural weaknesses** - parts of the business model that are fragile,
   commoditizing, or dependent on favorable conditions continuing
3. **Assumptions at risk** - what the bull case takes for granted that might not hold
4. **Permanent impairment scenarios** - what could structurally destroy
   earning power (not temporary dips - permanent damage)
5. **Hidden balance sheet risk** - off-balance-sheet liabilities, pension gaps,
   lease obligations, contingent liabilities
6. **Management risk** - where could leadership hurt shareholders
   (empire building, misaligned incentives, poor capital allocation)?
7. **What would prove the bear right** - specific, observable triggers
   (e.g., "margin compression below X% for 2+ consecutive quarters")

Be specific. Use numbers from filings, not vague concerns.
